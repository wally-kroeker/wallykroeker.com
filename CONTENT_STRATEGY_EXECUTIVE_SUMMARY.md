## Why Biweekly Works (vs. Weekly)

| Aspect | Weekly | Biweekly | Winner |
|--------|--------|----------|--------|
| Sustainability | Hard long-term | Natural rhythm | Biweekly |
| Client work | Suffers | Coexists | Biweekly |
| Quality | Variable | Consistent | Biweekly |
| SEO impact | Slow | Compounding | Same |
| Team size | Needs support | Solo works | Biweekly |
| Burnout risk | High | Low | Biweekly |
| Community growth | Faster | Still strong | Weekly |

**Biweekly is the right rhythm for a solo consultant with paid client work.**

If consulting revenue explodes, can increase to weekly. Until then, biweekly wins.

---

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk 1: Quality declines**
- Mitigation: Biweekly pace + 3-article buffer built in advance

**Risk 2: Consulting revenue overwhelms schedule**
- Mitigation: Content is marketing (ROI justifies time investment)

**Risk 3: Traffic doesn't grow**
- Mitigation: Patience (3-6 months to SEO payoff); success = consulting inquiries, not vanity traffic

**Risk 4: Community maintenance burden**
- Mitigation: Start small (50 members); community members help moderate; practitioner-led model

**Risk 5: Topic fatigue**
- Mitigation: 12-month topic backlog (Part 5 of main strategy); rotate across 4 pillars
