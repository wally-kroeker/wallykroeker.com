## Core Thesis

Commercial software doesn't die because someone builds a replacement from scratch. It dies because:

1. Open-source alternatives already exist at 80-90% feature parity (GIMP, Blender, Kdenlive, DaVinci Resolve, etc.)
2. AI-assisted coding makes it trivial to close the remaining 10-20% gap
3. Open codebases are accessible to AI contribution in ways proprietary code isn't
4. **The proprietary moat becomes a proprietary trap** — Adobe can't benefit from the AI contribution swarm without destroying their business model

## The Key Image

**"The floor rises."** It's not disruption from above (a better product). It's the baseline rising from below until the paid tier has nothing left to justify its price.

## Supporting Arguments

- Chinese AI models (DeepSeek, etc.) approaching frontier parity — possibly training on Western APIs
- Models commoditizing while getting more efficient (less compute needed)
- Microsoft doesn't need to build models — they own the infrastructure (data centers, Azure)
- But if efficiency breakthroughs continue, even the infrastructure moat shrinks
- Blender reportedly now has a solid video editor — one of many examples of open source closing gaps

## The Three Moats That Are Cracking

1. **Workflow lock-in** (Adobe Creative Cloud ecosystem) — AI agents can bridge/translate between ecosystems
2. **Professional edge cases** (codec support, hardware acceleration) — exactly the features AI can add to open codebases
3. **Enterprise support/SLA** — AI support agents already better than most Tier 1 vendor support

## The Deeper Implication

Software stops being a product and becomes a capability of intelligence. You don't buy Premiere. You describe what you want and intelligence assembles the workflow. **The tool becomes invisible. The intent becomes the interface.**

## Timeline Estimate

- Creative tools (Adobe, etc.): 3-5 years before bleeding is obvious, 7-10 before business model collapse
- Enterprise software (Salesforce, SAP): longer, due to regulation and data gravity
- Infrastructure (cloud compute): last to commoditize, but efficiency gains are chipping at it
