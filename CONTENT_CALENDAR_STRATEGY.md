## Part 2: Publishing Frequency & Rhythm

### Recommended Publishing Cadence: **Biweekly (Every 2 weeks)**

**Why biweekly?**
- Solo consultant has real client work (takes precedence)
- Quality over frequency (one solid 2,500-3,500 word article beats four shallow posts)
- Sustainable long-term (avoids burnout, maintains quality)
- Enough output to establish voice and expertise without overwhelming development
- Aligns with Cognitive Loop (personal writing practice already in weekly cadence; blog is monthly synthesis)

**Publishing schedule:**
- **Tuesday mornings** (9 AM CST): Article lands on wallykroeker.com/blog
- **Tuesday afternoon**: LinkedIn teaser + CTA to blog
- **Wednesday**: Email summary to newsletter (if list exists)
- **Thursday/Friday**: Twitter/X short-form threads (3-5 tweets pulling key insights)
- **End of week**: Build log update (if article required research or process documentation)

**Buffer strategy:**
- Draft 2-3 articles in advance (first sprint: write Month 2 content while Month 1 publishes)
- Evergreen content library (guides, checklists) published as-needed filler
- Quick-hit content (100-200 word reactions to news) don't count toward main cadence

### Format 3: Twitter/X Threads (Weekly)

**Process**:
1. Extract 3-5 key insights from published article
2. Frame as a teaching thread (not just promotion)
3. Thread format: 4-6 tweets, each self-contained insight
4. First tweet: hook question or bold claim
5. Middle tweets: supporting evidence/story/data
6. Last tweet: CTA (read full article, join GBAIC, retweet/reply)

**Example** (from "AI ROI Trap" article):
```
Thread: Why your AI pilot looks great in demos but flops in production. 5 things I've seen go wrong.

1/ Pilot conditions are fake. You have dedicated support, clean data, simple use cases.
Production? Messy. Fast. Millions of edge cases. Your model will see them and fail.

2/ The cost math doesn't scale. Pilot runs on 100 API calls/day. Production? 10,000.
Suddenly you're spending $2K/month on tokens instead of $50. Nobody budgeted for that.

3/ Your baseline is invisible. You optimized the pilot vs. itself.
But what was the unaugmented process doing? How much time did you actually save?
Without the baseline, you don't know if this works.

4/ Success metrics were designed to succeed.
Real metrics include: What breaks? Where does the model confidently give wrong answers?
What does support have to fix manually?

5/ You built a demo, not a system.
Demos don't need monitoring. Demos don't handle graceful degradation.
Demos don't log every decision for audit.
Systems do all of that. And suddenly the project budget triples.

Want the full analysis? I spent a week researching this.
→ [blog link]
```

## Part 11: Risk Mitigation & Sustainability

### Common Pitfalls (and how to avoid them)

**Risk 1: Quality decline** (publishing schedule overwhelms Wally)
- Mitigation: Biweekly (not weekly); 3-article buffer built in advance
- Mitigation: Filler content reduces pressure for weekly "big posts"
- Mitigation: If client work surges, extend to monthly temporarily (don't abandon)

**Risk 2: Topic fatigue** (running out of things to say)
- Mitigation: 12-month topic backlog (Part 5); rotate across pillars
- Mitigation: Community input (GBAIC); reader questions
- Mitigation: Short-form content (100 words) uses less mental energy

**Risk 3: Traffic doesn't grow** (content feels invisible)
- Mitigation: Patience (3-6 months to see SEO payoff)
- Mitigation: Active distribution (LinkedIn, Twitter, email) for first-month push
- Mitigation: Success metric isn't traffic; it's consulting inquiries + community growth
- Mitigation: Repurposing (same content in multiple formats = more visibility)

**Risk 4: Consulting revenue cannibalizes blog time**
- Mitigation: Content is marketing (invest in it as customer acquisition)
- Mitigation: If booked solid: use off-peak weeks for batch-writing articles
- Mitigation: Consider: would hiring help (contractor for content editing)?

**Risk 5: GBAIC becomes maintenance burden** (community grows faster than capacity)
- Mitigation: Start small (50 members in Slack/Discord)
- Mitigation: Community members moderate; Wally doesn't do all work
- Mitigation: "Practitioner-led" model (share ownership)

### Sustainability Checklist

- [ ] Biweekly rhythm feels sustainable after Month 2?
- [ ] Article quality hasn't declined?
- [ ] Client work isn't suffering?
- [ ] GBAIC growing without overwhelming Wally?
- [ ] Consulting pipeline improving (even if traffic is slow)?
- [ ] Enjoying the work? (This is critical)
